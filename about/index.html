My Life

I first learned to code in BASIC at the age of 13 on a VTECH Pre-Computer POWER PAD. In high school I wrote games for my TI-81 calculator; and by college I had mastered Excel Pivot Tables and dabbled with with Linux shell scripting.

I graduated from MIT in 2005 with a degree in Chemical Engineering. During my time there I worked summer research internships at Los Alamos National Laboratory and Proctor & Gamble and soon realized R&D wasnâ€™t my cup of tea. Not long afterwards I found out about the MIT E-Club and fell in love with entrepreneurship. Shortly thereafter, I co-founded Entreprelliance, an intercollegiate entrepreneurship organization; and CarboTran Technologies, a failed clean-energy startup.

Upon graduating from MIT, I had made my first website in PHP and began creating VBA Excel applications. I went on to found Pensero Group, a financial reporting and analysis consulting firm; and worked as a business consultant at MIDIOR Consulting helping clients develop new-product strategies.

Throughout this time, I developed database (MS Access and SQLite) applications, automated reporting solutions, and business intelligence systems. I also expanded my PHP and BASH scripting abilities.

Having lived in Cambridge, MA for ten years, I left the US in 2010 to work for Global Cycle Solutions (GCS) in Arusha, Tanzania. At GCS I worked on various projects including accounting and financial modeling, sales, marketing, strategy, ERP and reporting systems, employee training, and recruitment.

During this period, I found Python and fell in love with its simplicity and elegance. I soon replaced PHP with it as my language of choice for new script or web development projects. I use Python almost extensively for automating basic tasks, performing data analysis, and creating or integrating with APIs.

I left GCS in late 2012, to pursue freelancing opportunities in web and software development. Since then, I added offline-capable, responsive, web applications to my skill set.

In 2014, I founded the data analytics firm Nerevu Development.
